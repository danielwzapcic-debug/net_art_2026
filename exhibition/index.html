Banksy transformed art not through design or technique but by deconstructing what we thought art was in the first place. So much of his work hinged on the location and the experience of the work itself adding to the content. His graffiti style, although not novel, was starting to take over on a larger scale with provocative imagery as the artist pushed the boundaries of what art was previously. Net art and its rise also follows a similar trajectory, and by comparing the works of Banksy and various popular net artworks it is clear how in a way they fill the same need in society and even do so by employing similar techniques. These pieces all tell a story, not just in the standalone work but by the larger cultural impact which they carried and the motifs that they share, linking them within the larger zeitgeist. Overall Banksy is an innovative artist who was massively bolstered by the internet and the globalism of art which arose in part due to the net art movement.
