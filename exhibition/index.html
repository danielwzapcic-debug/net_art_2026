Banksy changed the art game not through design or technique but by deconstructing what we thought art was in the first place.

His work hinged on location and encounter. A stencil on a West Bank wall means something a gallery print never could. Net art works the same way. Olia Lialina’s fragmenting browser windows and JODI’s deliberately broken interfaces use their medium as the message, the same way Banksy uses the street itself.

Both movements came up in the 1990s without institutional permission. Both turn familiar symbols against themselves. Both put the audience in the position of stumbling into something they weren’t supposed to see, on a wall or in a browser tab left open too long.

Banksy’s reach grew on the internet. The same infrastructure net artists were busy taking apart. In a way they were all working on the same building, just from different sides of it.
